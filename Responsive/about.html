"Magic of Hogwarts

"Hogwarts is divided into four houses—Gryffindor, Hufflepuff, Ravenclaw, and Slytherin—each with its own values and personalities. The Sorting Hat assigns each new student to a house based on their traits. Hogwarts isn’t just a school; it's where the characters grow, form friendships, and discover who they are meant to be. With its enchanted ceiling in the Great Hall, ghostly residents like Nearly Headless Nick, and vast Forbidden Forest, Hogwarts is a magical place readers never forget."

About the Series

"Since the first book, Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone, debuted in 1997, J.K. Rowling’s magical saga has sold over 500 million copies, translated into over 80 languages, and inspired eight blockbuster movies. The series follows Harry as he learns of his true identity as a wizard and begins his schooling at Hogwarts. Alongside friends Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, Harry discovers a world hidden from non-magical eyes and confronts dark secrets about his past and his connection to the Dark Lord, Voldemort."

Character Guide

  • Harry Potter"The 'Boy Who Lived' is brave, determined, and often quick to act. Though orphaned and raised by his unkind relatives, the Dursleys, Harry learns about his heritage and his parents' legacy upon turning eleven. Through every trial, Harry learns valuable lessons about trust, self-sacrifice, and the importance of standing up for others—even when it means facing unimaginable dangers."
  • Hermione Granger the brightest witch of her age, possessing both academic brilliance and a fierce sense of justice. Raised by non-magical parents, she immerses herself in learning about the wizarding world and champions the rights of house-elves and other magical beings. As part of the trio, her logical mind and steadfast courage are invaluable, helping them solve countless mysteries along the way.
  • Ron Weaslethe sixth son in a large family of wizards, is humorous, loyal, and unafraid of challenges. While he occasionally feels overshadowed, his bravery and unwavering support for his friends never waver. His character shows the importance of friendship, humility, and self-confidence, and his moments of heroism reveal his deep loyalty.
